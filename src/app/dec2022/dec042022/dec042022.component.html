Introduction

Regarding technology, you want to ensure you get the most out of your investments. IT expense projects are a great way to do this because they often don't require large investments upfront. Instead, these projects are designed to improve efficiencies in the company and make things easier for employees. However, there is a lot more opportunity here than just improving efficiency and making life easier for employees; turning expense projects into revenue-generating products is one way companies can see actual returns on their technology investments.

Leverage your expense projects

When working on a technology expense project, you want to ensure you get the most out of it. Whether it's a new system, new software, or a new network, there are a few things that should be in place before you sign off on any project:

The technology needs to meet your business goals and objectives. Even though the technology is available, it will still work for your situation. Sometimes, you may need to wait until better solutions become available or develop something custom-tailored for your company's needs (for example, if the standard solution works better with your existing processes).

The right people need to be involved in these project's decision-making processes and implementation phases to ensure they'll be successful once they go live. Choose people who have experience implementing similar systems within other companies. Hence, they know what challenges will arise during implementation and can help avoid them as much as possible before starting work on this particular project management effort!

Make it easy to use and purchase

If you have a product or service that can be sold, you should make it easy for your customers to buy. Here are some things you can do:

Use a simple, intuitive interface. Make sure your website is easy to navigate and use. Also, make sure that any other channels through which you're selling (e.g., websites like eBay or Craigslist) are organized in an intuitive way that makes it easy for people who have yet to become familiar with them to purchase your product or service.

Provide a single point of contact for purchasing and customer service. Suppose there are multiple ways for people to buy from you (e.g., online shopping cart vs. emailing in requests). In that case, they may get confused about where they need to go to start using your product/service as well as how much money they need to spend on what components of the offering at different times during the purchase process (i.e., when do I need more storage space?). If this happens often enough, customers might decide not to bother trying anymore because they want to save time figuring out the pricing structure when they want an answer right away.

Communicate the benefits to customers

It is essential to communicate the benefits of the new product or service to customers. Make sure they understand how it will save them time and money or increase their productivity.

Get feedback from users

This is another step that can be done in parallel with the development of your product, but the earlier you do it, the better. As soon as you have something usable, get feedback from users. It's essential to get feedback from people who are going to use your product because they will help make improvements that are more relevant and valuable (especially if they're paying for it).

Use surveys (like this one) or interviews to find out what problems people have with using technology in their workplace and how they would fix them. You can also ask them what kind of information they'd like from an application like yours and how often they would use it.

Once you've collected data about what users want from a product like yours, use that information to create a backlog of potential features for future releases. Promote it within your company

You can market your product to generate revenue, but be careful not to over-market. This is a good idea if you're sending out press releases and making a big deal about it. However, if people need to learn your product and why they need it, they won't buy it.

The best way to get the word out is through public relations (PR). Think of PR as communicating with customers by getting news stories about the benefits of your products and services in publications that cater to those customers' needs.

Converting IT projects into revenue-generating products is a great way to see the ROI for technology initiatives. For example, if you have an open-source project and other companies use it, you can charge them for support or customization services. You could sell the product as-is or under a commercial license with additional features and functionality.

Conclusion

Converting IT projects into revenue generators is a great way to see the ROI for technology initiatives. The key is to consider how you can leverage your current investment, make it easy for customers to use and purchase, communicate the benefits, get user feedback, and promote it within your company.
