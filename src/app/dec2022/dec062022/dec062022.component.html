Introduction

It's true: social media has changed the world. And while it's easy to think of Facebook as just a place to post photos of whatever you had for lunch, there's an entire world of opportunities beneath the surface. Social media can be a great way to build your audience and connect with them on a personal level, but only if you use it correctly. Here are some tips for using Facebook effectively and building your audience:

Audit your Facebook profile

We'll start by auditing your Facebook profile, which will help you understand who your ideal audience is and what makes them tick. For now, think of the people interested in reading your blog or buying/using your product.

  • Who are they?
  • How old are they?
  • What gender are they?
  • What kind of interests do they have? (sports, fashion, cooking)
  • Use the correct language on Facebook

It would help if you used the language your audience uses, but make sure you're using it in a way that aligns with your brand and what you're trying to communicate. In other words, if you're selling products or services to moms, don't talk about how excellent your app is for kids—talk about how much easier it makes life for busy parents!

Be consistent

Consistency is key. Once you've built an audience for your page, you need to keep them engaged by posting content regularly. The best way to do this is by planning a schedule that includes posting times and topics for each day of the week. For example, if you have a beauty blog and want to post about your favorite beauty products every Sunday, then you can plan it out so that they go up on Sundays at 4 PM EST or 9 AM PST or whatever works best for where you live. Posting daily can be hard initially, but once it becomes second nature, it'll be easy and fun!

Keep in mind that consistency doesn't mean posting constantly. While it's good to post often enough that people know what they should expect when they follow your page (and when they see your posts), don't overdo it. Too much communication can lead some followers away from wanting anything more than just an occasional peek into what goes on in your world (and we're not talking about stalking). You also don't want to bombard them with updates every time there's a new picture of their kid drinking juice—they probably don't care as much as their friend does—but showing off those cute little fingers grasping at straws during childhood development would probably make for some great content. And remember: consistency means everything when building an audience online; if something changes drastically from month to month, no one knows how often any given update will occur going forward unless otherwise noted (or else imagine trying to build trust. So keep things simple whenever possible -- especially if aiming toward long-term success rather than short-term gains.

Turn yourself into a hashtag

Hashtags are a great way to engage with your audience, but unfortunately, many people need to use them correctly. Hashtags can be used to promote your content and join a conversation. Make sure you use the correct hashtag and keep all topics distinct from one post. For example: if you're discussing healthcare reform, use the hashtag #healthcarereform. If you're posting a photo of yourself at work, use the hashtags #worklife and #workinghard.

Join conversations with other pages

Join in the conversation. Don't just promote your own content; be sure to respond to comments and questions from other users.

Be genuine and authentic. Don't be a spammer! Nobody likes those who post nothing but links to their own websites or products, so don't do it either.

Ask questions when appropriate. You won't learn anything if you never ask anyone for help! Even if you don't need help, asking questions about related topics will get people interested in talking about them with you on Facebook (and might even lead them back to your site). And if someone asks a question that prompts an answer from one of your pages or allows interaction between two of the pages' followers (for example: "my dog has fleas - how do I get rid of them?", jump right in! The more conversations there are going on between different Facebook users across multiple pages, the better off all those pages' owners will be because they'll all benefit from each other's audiences being exposed to different types of content through these interactions.

Cross-promote your content on other channels

Now that you've built a following on Facebook, it's time to start cross-promoting your content. This means sharing the same content on other social media channels—but not in the same way you shared it on Facebook. For example, if you published a blog post about "How to be successful in business" and used #success in your text, don't use #success again when promoting this post on Twitter; instead try something like #business or even just simply include the link to your blog post and use no hashtags at all.

The key is consistency; you want potential customers who see one piece of content from you to know exactly where else they can find more information about what interests them most based solely on what channel they choose (Facebook vs Instagram vs Pinterest). And since these posts will only be seen by those who have connected with either yourself or your business page or blog via those channels already, there shouldn't be any issue with sharing links directly within an account itself.

Social media is a great way to connect and build an audience

It can be time-consuming, but it is worth it. You can use social media to build your list or to promote your products. You can even use it to build relationships with your audience.

Conclusion

Social media is an excellent way to connect with your audience. You don't need to spend hours on it every day; make sure you do it consistently over time, and you will see results. It's also great to build a larger audience and grow your business.
